Personal Life

Robert Greene resides in Los Angeles with his partner, filmmaker Anna Biller. In 2018, he suffered a stroke that affected his mobility and speech, but he has continued to write and inspire others with his resilience and determination.

The 48 Laws of Power

Published in 1998, The 48 Laws of Power is a modern manual for understanding and mastering power. It presents 48 timeless principles, such as "Never Outshine the Master" and "Assume Formlessness," illustrated with historical examples.

This book has sold over 1.2 million copies and is celebrated for its strategic insights, making it a must-read for anyone interested in the dynamics of power and influence.

The Art of Seduction

Published in 2001, The Art of Seduction delves into the timeless techniques of persuasion and attraction. Greene identifies archetypes of seducers, such as the Siren and the Charmer, and offers strategies to captivate and influence others.

Enriched with stories of historical figures like Cleopatra and Casanova, this book serves as a fascinating exploration of human behavior and the art of seduction.

Other Works by Robert Greene

  • The 33 Strategies of War (2006): A guide to applying military strategies in personal and professional life.
  • The 50th Law (2009): Co-written with 50 Cent, focusing on fearlessness as the key to success.
  • Mastery (2012): Discusses achieving excellence by understanding the process of mastery.
  • The Laws of Human Nature (2018): Examines human motivations and behaviors.

Robert Greene's Legacy

Greene's works have been translated into dozens of languages and have influenced readers worldwide, including entrepreneurs, artists, and political leaders. Despite facing criticism for exploring manipulation and power, his books remain invaluable guides for understanding human nature and achieving personal success.
